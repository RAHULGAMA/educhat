What is MBBS Course?

MBBS full form in medical is Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery. MBBS course duration is 5.5 years with 5 years of academic study and 6 months of internship. An MBBS course is an undergraduate medical degree awarded to students who have completed their training in the field of medicine and surgery. The MBBS course is recognized in many countries as the standard qualification for medical doctors and physicians.
MBBS course is the dream career choice for students across India. Over 18 lakh candidates aspire to pursue an MBBS course and sit for NEET UG as per the latest data. The MBBS course is a professional degree required to practise Medicine, diagnose ailments, give medication and perform surgery. There are 91,927 NEET UG seats available for MBBS course, with 48,012 (52%) in government medical colleges and 43,915 (48%) in private medical colleges.

Why Study an MBBS Course?

  • Helping Others: A fundamental reason many people choose to become doctors is the opportunity to make a positive impact on people's lives. Medical professionals can diagnose, treat, and prevent illnesses, improving the health and well-being of patients.
  • Job Stability and Demand:The healthcare industry has a consistently high demand for medical professionals. As the global population continues to grow and age, the need for healthcare services, including doctors, remains strong.
  • Respected Profession:Being a doctor is widely respected in society. Doctors are considered experts in their field and play a crucial role in saving lives and maintaining public health.
  • Diverse Specializations:The field of medicine offers a wide range of specializations to choose from, including Surgery, Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Radiology, Cardiology, Dermatology, and more.
  • Intellectual Challenge:Medicine is a dynamic field that requires critical thinking, problem-solving, and continuous learning. The challenges and complexities involved in diagnosing and treating patients can be intellectually stimulating.
  • Financial Rewards:Medicine is often associated with solid earning potential. While the cost of medical education can be significant, the potential for financial stability and a rewarding income over the long term can be appealing.

Through MBBS course, the university seeks to equip all MBBS students with a modern, growth-driven education. A Bachelor of Medicine and a Bachelor of Surgery degree qualify graduates for the health industry.

MBBS Specialisations
Ophthalmology Ophthalmology is a medical surgical discipline concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of eye problems.
Orthopaedics Orthopaedic surgery, sometimes known as orthopaedics, is the discipline of surgery concerned with musculoskeletal problems.
Anesthesiology The medical speciality of anesthesiology, sometimes known as anaesthesiology or anaesthesia, is concerned with the whole perioperative treatment of patients before, during, and after surgery.
Dermatology Dermatology is the branch of medicine that deals with skin problems.
Psychiatry Psychiatry is a medical speciality concerned with the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of mental illnesses.
General medicine General medicine treats and manages a wide range of long-term ailments, such as diabetes and respiratory disorders, as well as shorter-term health issues, such as skin conditions.
General Surgery The branch of surgery that deals with the most common surgical treatments. General surgeons specialise in the treatment of disorders of the belly, breast, head and neck, blood vessels, and digestive tract.
Obstetrics and Gynaecology Obstetrics and Gynaecology is the medical speciality that comprises both obstetrics and gynaecology.
ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) Otorhinolaryngology is a surgical specialisation of medicine that deals with the surgical and medicinal care of head and neck diseases.
Paediatrics Paediatrics is the medical speciality concerned with the medical care of infants, children, adolescents, and young adults

NEET UG Cut Off

The cutoff for MBBS in NEET UG refers to the minimum score that a candidate must achieve to secure admission into an MBBS course through NEET.
The cutoff for MBBS in NEET UG varies each year and is determined by factors such as the difficulty level of the exam, the number of candidates appearing for the exam, and the number of available seats. The cutoff is different for different categories of students, including General, OBC, SC, ST, and PwD (Persons with Disabilities) .
